Bara

Bara is a Newari snack. It is commonly known as 'Wo' in Newari language. This traditional food among Newari people symbolizes good luck during festivals, birthdays, weddings and other occasions. In appearance, it looks like a savory rice-flour pancake. It is served with Newari drinks like 'Aila'(Local Spirit) and 'Chya-ang'(Local Sake). It is made from lentils paste after it is mixed with garlic ginger, turmeric and salt. It can be eaten with eggs or minced meat on the top.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

5-10 min.

serving

4-5 servings

instructions

step 1

Soak lentils in a large bowl of water overnight.

step 2

Grind it finely next day.

step 3

Add 1-2 tbsp of water to make smooth batter if needed. It shouldnâ€™t be in the liquid form or very thick.

step 4

Once the batter is ready, add salt, turmeric, garlic and ginger paste.

step 5

Whisk it for a couple of minutes.

step 6

Heat 1/2 tbsp of oil in a non-stick pan or cast-iron pan.

step 7

Slowly pour the paste into the pan and spread it to make a circle shape with the spatula. Or, add some oil on your palm, take some paste in your palm and make it into a circle before putting it into the hot oil.

step 8

When one side turns a bit brown, turn over to the other side.

step 9

When both sides are brown it is ready.

step 10

Serve hot with a drink.

ingredients

2 Cups of Moong lentils

1 tbsp Garlic & Ginger

1/2 tbsp Salt

1/2 Turmeric

1/2 Cup oil

50-100 grams Minced buff meat (optional for topping)

Egg (optional for topping)

tools

Cooking Pan (Non-stick/Cast iron)

Stapula

Medium sized Mixing Bowl
