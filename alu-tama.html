Alu Tama / Chhon Kwa

Chhon Kwa is one of the most popular and unique Newari dishes. Chhon means bamboo shoot and Kwa means hot soup or stew. The dish is famously known as Alu-Tama in Nepal and is also known among Newari community as Newari curry stew. It is prepared with potatoes, bamboo shoots (made from fermented bamboo) and black-eyed beans. The soup is mainly sour and spicy in taste. The dish is a gluten-free food hence an alternate source for a gluten-free meal. It is served as one of the important side dishes in Samay Baji set.

prep time

10-15min.

cook time

20-25 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Soak black-eyed beans overnight and drain the water or can(if you use canned black-eyed beans).

step 2

Boil potatoes and black-eyed beans.

step 3

Heat oil in a saucepan or a cooking pot.

step 4

Once the oil is hot, fry cumin seeds and onions until it goes brown.

step 5

Add boiled black-eyed beans and potatoes, mix and fry for 2-3 mins.

step 6

Add turmeric, cumin, sliced green chillies, garlic-ginger paste and salt.

step 7

Once there is a spicy aroma, add bamboo shoots and mix well with all the spices.

step 8

Add 4 cups of boiling water and cook it for 10 mins over medium-low heat.

step 9

Sprinkle some chopped fresh coriander before serving.

step 10

Serve with rice, beaten-rice, dinks or Samay Baji set.

ingredients

200 grams of Red Potato

1 cup black-eyed peas

1-2pcs chopped Onion and Tomatoes

1 Cup bamboo shoots

1tsp Turmeric Powder, Cumin powder

2 tbsp Mustard oil

Salt

2 tbsp Ginger/Garlic paste

2 Sliced green chillies

20 grams Coriander leaves

tools

Cooking Pot

Stapula

Bowl

Ladle
